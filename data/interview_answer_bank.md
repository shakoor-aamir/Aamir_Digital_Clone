## 8. Automotive architecture (high level)
Modern automotive architecture is moving toward centralized and software-defined systems.

Instead of many distributed ECUs, there’s a shift toward domain controllers and zonal architectures, improving scalability and updateability.

This also enables faster software development and better integration across vehicle functions.

---

## 9. AUTOSAR
AUTOSAR provides a standardized architecture for automotive software.

In Classic AUTOSAR, the system is layered—application, RTE, and basic software.

This separation helps with modularity, reuse, and integration across suppliers.

From a product perspective, it helps manage complexity and ensures consistency across platforms.

## 10. ASPICE / SWE2
ASPICE focuses on structured development processes.

At SWE2 level, the focus is on software architectural design—defining components, interfaces, and data flow.

As a Product Manager, I ensure requirements are clear and traceable so that teams can design and implement effectively within this framework.
